# superstore-sales-dashboard
Excel dashboard analysing 300+ sales records: regional performance &amp; discount impact on profit.
Superstore Sales Dashboard
Dataset Description
This dataset contains 300+ sales records from a retail superstore, covering transactions between 2011 and 2014. Each row represents a single line item from an order and includes:

Order details – Order ID, Order Date, Ship Date, Ship Mode

Customer information – Customer Name, Segment (Consumer, Corporate, Home Office)

Geographic data – Country, City, State, Postal Code, Region (Central, East, South, West)

Product details – Product ID, Category (Furniture, Office Supplies, Technology), Sub‑Category, Product Name

Financial metrics – Sales (USD), Quantity, Discount (0 to 0.8 = 0% to 80%), Profit (USD)

The data reflects real‑world sales performance and allows analysis of regional trends, product profitability, and the impact of discounts.

Key Insights
1. Top Region by Sales & Profit
The West region generated the highest total sales, followed by the East and Central regions.

The West region also contributed the largest share of overall profit, indicating strong market performance.

2. Discount Effect on Profit
A scatter plot of Discount vs. Profit reveals that discounts above 30% often lead to negative profit (losses).

While moderate discounts (0–20%) can increase sales volume without hurting profitability, deep discounts erode margins and should be applied selectively.

3. Product Category Performance
Office Supplies had the highest total sales volume but moderate profit margins.

Technology products delivered the highest profit per unit, whereas some Furniture items showed losses due to heavy discounting.

4. Top Customers
A small number of customers (≈5%) account for a disproportionate share of total sales – a classic Pareto pattern. Targeting these high‑value customers with loyalty programmes could further increase revenue.

Tools Used
Microsoft Excel – for all data manipulation and visualisation tasks:

PivotTables – summarised sales and profit by region, category, and customer.

Charts – bar charts, scatter plots, and column charts to visualise trends.

Dashboard – arranged multiple charts on a single sheet for a consolidated view.

No coding required – all operations performed using built‑in Excel features.
